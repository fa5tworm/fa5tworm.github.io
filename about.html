About Me

Hello there! I'm Otmane, and I'm a passionate Rust programmer who loves to explore the world of coding, reading, and personal growth.

As a Rust enthusiast, I'm captivated by the language's focus on safety, performance, and concurrency. I find great joy in writing clean, efficient, and reliable code that adheres to Rust's best practices and design patterns. Whether I'm working on low-level system programming or high-level web development, Rust has become an indispensable tool in my arsenal.

But my interests extend far beyond just coding. I'm an avid reader, with a particular fondness for books that challenge my perspectives and expand my understanding of the world. From thought-provoking non-fiction to captivating poetry, I'm always on the lookout for new literary adventures.

Staying active and connected with nature is also a crucial part of my life. I love to go jogging, exploring the great outdoors, and finding solace in the tranquility of natural spaces. It's during these moments that I'm able to recharge and find inspiration for my creative pursuits.

Music is another passion of mine, and I have a deep appreciation for the emotive power of electronic dance music and trance. I find that immersing myself in these genres helps me to tap into my creative flow and gain new perspectives on the world around me.

In addition to these personal interests, I'm also deeply committed to making a positive impact on the world. I'm the creator of readingdb.org, a website dedicated to helping Latino communities improve their English reading skills. I'm also a strong advocate for open-web data privacy and security, as well as human rights.

Through my involvement in these initiatives, I strive to use my skills and knowledge to make a tangible difference in the lives of others. I believe that by fostering a more inclusive, secure, and equitable digital landscape, we can empower individuals and communities to thrive.

When I'm not immersed in my various pursuits, you might find me watching thought-provoking late-night shows or documentaries, or scavenging GitHub for new and exciting projects to explore. I'm always eager to learn and grow, and I find great fulfillment in sharing my knowledge and experiences with others.

If you're interested in collaborating with me on a Rust project, feel free to reach out to me on Twitter (@otechai). I'm always excited to work with like-minded individuals and to contribute to the Rust ecosystem.
